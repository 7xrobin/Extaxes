What is Sparerpauschbetrag & Vorabpauschale?

Germany gives you €1,000/year tax-free on investment gains (€2,000 if married) — this is called the Sparerpauschbetrag. Accumulating ETFs also owe a small annual advance tax each January called the Vorabpauschale — your German broker handles this automatically by debiting your cash account. All figures are estimates — not tax advice.

★ Why accumulating ETFs are more tax-efficient

An accumulating ETF (thesaurierend) reinvests dividends internally — so you owe no annual dividend tax. You only pay capital gains tax when you sell. Additionally, all ETFs receive a 30% tax exemption (Teilfreistellung, §20 InvStG), reducing the effective rate from 26.375% to ~18.46%. Individual stocks and savings products receive no such exemption and are taxed at the full 26.375%.
